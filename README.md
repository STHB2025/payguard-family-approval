# 🛡️ PayGuard

### Family approval for high-risk payments using programmable banking concepts.

PayGuard is a prototype that explores how programmable banking can create safer financial experiences through trusted approval workflows.

Instead of allowing risky transactions to happen instantly, PayGuard introduces a simple human trust layer where a trusted contact can approve or decline high-risk payments before funds leave the account.

---

# 💡 The Problem

Many vulnerable users — especially seniors — face financial scams, mistaken transfers, and risky payment behaviour.

Banks can send alerts after transactions happen, but there is often no simple way for families or trusted contacts to intervene before money is sent.

---

# ✅ The Solution

PayGuard creates a lightweight approval flow for high-risk payments.

If a transaction matches certain risk conditions (example: large amount or new beneficiary), the system:

1. Flags the payment as high risk
2. Sends an approval request
3. Allows a trusted contact to approve or block the payment
4. Updates the payment status in real time

---

# ⚙️ Features

- 📊 Dashboard with payment stats
- ⚠️ High-risk payment detection
- ✅ One-click approve/decline flow
- 📱 WhatsApp-style approval simulation
- 🧾 Payment history log
- 🇿🇦 Rand currency support and local-first UX

---

# 🔌 Programmable Banking Concept

This project explores how programmable banking events and transaction workflows could trigger trusted approval systems in real time.

The prototype demonstrates how programmable banking could support:

- Family oversight
- Senior financial safety
- Shared payment decision-making
- Fraud reduction workflows

---

# 💰 Monetisation Potential

Potential business models include:

- Family protection subscriptions
- Fintech safety integrations
- Care facility dashboards
- SME payment approval workflows

---

# 🧠 Why This Matters

PayGuard is not designed to replace banks.

It explores how trust and human verification can become part of digital financial experiences — especially for vulnerable users and community-focused environments.
